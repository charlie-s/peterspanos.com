Peter Spanos

Peter Spanos is CEO of Vetta Sports, an amateur sports participation business. Vetta owns and operates seven indoor sports facilities in the St. Louis, Missouri metropolitan area and provides sports programming at several outdoor venues.

Pete played Division I soccer, and earned an MBA prior to founding Vetta Sports. Driven by his passion to learn and teach, Vetta became a place where people of all ages play and learn as athletes, making friendships in the process. Youth sports instruction were Vetta’s building blocks; adult leagues, youth leagues, camps, parties and concessions round out today’s core offerings.

Vetta is a family enterprise in the truest sense with Pete’s brother/partner John naming the company after their grandmother. Vetta has a long term committed management team with 300 FTEs and 2million visits per year. The team has operated numerous sports such as soccer, tennis, racquetball, volleyball, basketball, lacrosse, and fitness centers.

Mr. Spanos spends time in management development for different organizations such as lead management instructor for the US Indoor Sports Arena Operator’s certification.

St. Louis County Soccer Complex is a newly constructed 13 field synthetic turf field Complex provided by St. Louis County. It is expected to attract tens of millions of dollars in annual economic activity for the St. Louis region. Vetta Sports’ role is to seek and maximize the economic opportunities for the new Complex while managing its operational functions.

Stadia Ventures Sports Fund initial investor and selection committee. Stadia Ventures is a global sports innovation hub based in St. Louis for connecting entrepreneurs, industry partners, and investors. Companies from all over the country come to St. Louis to participate in the Stadia ecosystem for sports innovation growth.

America Scores St. Louis founding Board Chairman of the local not-for-profit urban soccer and writing after school program. Over 2,000 children have participated in the program from both St. Louis City and County public elementary schools.

St. Nicholas Church Past Co-Chairman of Growth, Development and Construction. The successful voting strategy promoted both construction of a St. Louis County Family Life Center and a renovation of the city sanctuary.
